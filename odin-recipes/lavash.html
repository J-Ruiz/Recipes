Lavash

Lavash is an Armenian flatbread that can be folded and manipulated to fit whatever circumstances you can imagine. It can be the bread of a sandwich, or eaten plainly with a dip...or by itself with nothing else at all! Traditional recipes for Lavash are made in a brick or wood oven, however it can be made on the stove top with a pan. The main difference between Lavash and flatbread is the texture of the breads. Lavash is a softer, foldable bread when compareds to flatbread. Try this recipe out and find just how delicious Lavash can be!

    Ingredients
  • Warm Water
  • Milk
  • Vegetable Oil (Or any neutral oil)
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Instant Yeast
  • Flour
    Steps
  1. Mix all ingredients EXCEPT THE FLOUR in a large boil
  2. Add flour to the mixture slowly and mix as you continue to add flour until the dough forms
  3. Make a giant dough ball with a light brushing of oil. Place the doughball into large bowl and cover it with plastic wrap Place it into the oven for 40 minutes (this is to prevent wind and drafts from destroying the mixture)
  4. Divide the dough into about 20 pieces and shape each one into a ball. Cover with a small towel to prevent drying out
  5. Heat a pan(preferably non stick. Coat lightly with cooking spray) using medium heat. Flour a surface and your rolling pin and roll out the dough thinly
  6. Place rolled out dough into heated pan and cook for 45-60 seconds until bubbles form. Flip it and cook the other side for 45 seconds or until cooked Remove the bread into a plan and cook the remaining dough mixtures remembering to roll out each.
