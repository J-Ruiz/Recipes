Lavash

Lavash is an Armenian flatbread that can be folded and manipulated to fit whatever circumstances you can imagine. It can be the bread of a sandwich, or eaten plainly with a dip...or by itself with nothing else at all! Traditional recipes for Lavash are made in a brick or wood oven, however it can be made on the stove top with a pan. The main difference between Lavash and flatbread is the texture of the breads. Lavash is a softer, foldable bread when compareds to flatbread. Try this recipe out and find just how delicious Lavash can be!
